The Four Facets of Psychic Rumination

    1. Relaxation

    Psychic rumination requires a deep level of relaxation in order to be effective. This can be achieved through cubic breathing, also known as "four-square breathing". This is a meditative technique that is used to set ones mind and begin the production of Alpha waves (8-12 hz). The technique involves inhaling for a count of four, holding the breath for a count of four, exhaling for a count of four, and then holding the breath again for a count of four. This process is repeated for several minutes, allowing the individual to focus on their breath and release any tension or stress.

    2. Intention

    This involves setting a clear intention or goal for the rumination, such as seeking a specific knowledge or information. This intention should be unambiguous, in order to achieve the desired outcome. Instead of having a general intention like "I want to communicate with aliens," try to be more specific such as "I want to receive information about alien methods of mathematics”. It is also important to be use positive language when setting your intention.

    3. Energy Alignment

    This involves aligning your energy with the energy of the extraterrestrial beings, so that communication is more easily established. This facet is the most difficult for beginners. In order to successfully align your energy, it's important to work on developing your intuition and sensitivity to energy. A powerful technique for this purpose is sensory deprivation, more about this is explained below. Its also important to cultivate a strong sense of trust and belief in your ability to connect with extraterrestrial beings through psychic rumination.

    4. Receptivity

    opening ones self to be receptive to the knowledges received is very important. This involves opening your mind and being receptive to any messages or communication that may come through. This is a passive state of being, where you allow the information to come to you, rather than trying to actively seek it out.

Sensory Deprivation

The Ganzfeld experiment in parapsychology was inspired by the work of German psychologist Wolfgang Metzger, who first demonstrated the perceptual distortions that can occur when a person is presented with a homogeneous visual field. In the 1970s, Charles Honorton at the Maimonides Medical Center sought to study the connection between ESP and dreams by exposing subjects to sensory deprivation in the Ganzfeld state, with the hypothesis that this would enhance "psi-conductive states" and improve the transmission of "psi-mediated information". The first full Ganzfeld experiment was published by Honorton and Sharon Harper in 1974, and since then, Ganzfeld experiments have been a staple in parapsychological research.
